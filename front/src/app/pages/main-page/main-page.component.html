Solving the cancer together
This site allows you to use the a generative deep learning model that can generate new ligands against chronic myeloid leukemia.
Problem
Chronic myeloid leukemia is a very dangerous disease. About 15% of all leukemia is CML. The American Cancer Societyâ€™s estimates for chronic myeloid leukemia in the US for 2022 are:
  • About 8,860 new cases will be diagnosed with CML
  • About 1,220 people will die of CML
About 1 person in 526 will get CML in their lifetime in the US. And currently there is no medicine that would effectively combat this disease. The process of drug development is very time-consuming and expensive. Currently, there are several types of inhibitors, but they are not able to block any mutations of leukemia. And although scientists are actively working to find a solution, at the moment the problem remains unresolved.
Solution
One of the possible solutions that will speed up the process of finding the necessary medicine is a combined approach: simultaneous use of computer modeling methods and generative neural networks. The purpose of our work is to generate in this way a multikinase inhibitor, which will be unique for every mutation.
